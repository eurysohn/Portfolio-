APICS Introduction to Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP) Key Discussion Points:  Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP) Definition: ‐ A process to develop tactical plans that provide management the ability to strategically direct its businesses to achieve competitive advantage on a continuous basis by integrating customer-focused marketing plans for new and existing products with the management of the supply chain. The process brings together all the plans for the business (sales, marketing, development, manufacturing, sourcing, and financial) into one integrated set of plans. APICS Dictionary, 14th edition  S&OP Processes: Product/ Brand Plan Marketing Plan Sales Plan Demand Forecast S&OP Demand Management Demand Planning Supply Planning Production Resources Inventory Distribution Production Plan Master Scheduling Detailed Planning and Scheduling Facilities Planning Labor Planning Machine Planning Investment Targets Channel Distribution Shipment Plan Transportation Warehousing Labor and Equipment  Objectives of S&OP: ‐ S&OP supports and measures the progress of the entire organization in meeting the enterprise's business plan. ‐ S&OP ensures that the strategic plans developed in the business plan are realistic and can be successfully executed before they are passed to tactical planning. ‐ S&OP enables the organization to successfully manage marketplace change. ‐ S&OP provides for the effective management of finished goods inventories and order backlog to support customer service. ‐ S&OP reporting mechanisms enable the S&OP planning teams to identify when actual performance has deviated from the plan. ‐ The S&OP process enables the entire management team to understand the challenges confronting the organization and to act upon them in proper priority or focus.
