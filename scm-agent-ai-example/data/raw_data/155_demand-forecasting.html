    The Datamine Guide to Demand Forecasting

    Predicting customer demand is critical, but it’s not always straightforward.  Done well, demand forecasting can help streamline operations, reduce waste, and meet customer expectations.  Done poorly, it can cost you sales, impact your inventory, and hurt your bottom line.

    Your step-by-step guide to demand forecasting in business

    For businesses across Australia and New Zealand, accurately forecasting demand can be the difference between profitability and missed opportunities.

    Our guide outlines a clear framework for demand forecasting optimisation.  You’ll learn the key factors that influence demand, explore the most effective methodologies and techniques, and walk through practical steps to build a forecasting cycle that works.

    Interested?  Please enter your details below to download a copy of the Datamine Guide to Demand Forecasting.

    HERE'S WHAT'S INSIDE

    What is demand forecasting?

    You’ll learn how forecasting helps align your stock levels with real customer behaviour, avoid costly over or under-stocking, and improve decision-making across operations, supply chain and staffing.

     

    Business challenges when forecasting demand

    Even experienced businesses can struggle with demand forecasting.  We outline the most common challenges – from relying on outdated tools and using inaccurate or inconsistent data to underestimating the need for strategy and internal buy-in.  Learn how to overcome these pitfalls to get more accurate, actionable forecasts.

     

    Internal and external factors that influence demand

    A single variable rarely shapes demand.  We explore the wide range of factors that affect forecasting accuracy, including shelf life, supply chain delays, warehousing capacity, seasonality, competitor activity and economic shifts.  You’ll also find a list of questions to help you assess what influences demand in your specific business.

     

    Forecasting methods and techniques

    There’s no one-size-fits-all approach to demand forecasting.  Learn about core forecasting methodologies – qualitative, time-series and causal forecasting – and when to use each technique.  You’ll discover how to choose the right model for your needs based on data availability, time horizon and business goals.

     

    5 steps to effective forecasting

    To get forecasting right, you need a repeatable system.  We walk you through our five clearly defined steps to set up your forecasting process.  It’s a complete cycle you can embed into your operations for ongoing optimisation.

     

    See forecasting in action

    Read a real-world example from one of our clients.  This major retailer partnered with Datamine to centralise and streamline its forecasting approach, leading to an 8% increase in sales and a 17% drop in waste.  The takeaway?  The right forecasting model can quickly deliver measurable results.
